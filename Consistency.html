Consistency and Standards


"Users often choose system functions by mistake and will need a clearly marked "emergency exit" to leave the unwanted state without having to go through an extended dialogue. Support undo and redo." - Nielsen


We covered quite a bit of this in the Visibility section, just because the two inter lap, but just because the site quality is so poor, I really can't emphasize enough how much I dislike the consistency on this website. From the navigation to the redundancy of several rows in some of the pages, this site has a difficult time, winning my personal appraisal for the consistency. Another thing I noticed was that there is a sub-navigation existent throughout pats of the site, that allows you to look up info about news, media, about us, faculty assistance, etc. But if you didn't realize "About Us" now exists in two different places, what's up? So I looked into the issue with a little more curiosity. Guess what I found? The two pages are completely unrelated. Seriously a user should never have to wonder what's going on with two separate pages in the sites navigation.

The issue with the Current Students page being the Student Life, is truly misleading and violates the heuristic for consistency in so many ways. Also there are multiple rows of the same info on different pages. For example Academics and Student Life/Current Students both have the same Academic Support row. I don't like it when I go to the research home page, and it is completely different from the home site. It is confusing and new to me, user shouldn't have to adjust to the new website.
