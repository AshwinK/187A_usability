Visibility


"The system should always keep users informed about what is going on, through appropriate feedback within reasonable time." - Nielsen


This site has a consistent top bar navigation but it doesn't tell you where you are usually, unless you look at the banner or deduce from the content on the page. The sidebar navigation isn't consistent and the sub-navigation under content such as President's Message, doesn't give very useful clues as how to get back to the original About Us page Turns out you have to use the homepage to get back.

However, the top bar navigation is generally very well preserved, even if there are some very confusing sidebar and sub-navigation issues. However, the library link is very different, in that there isn't a consistent navigation, nor is there a way to properly reach the home site without using the footer navigation, or browser buttons, a huge visibility issue.

I personally dislike how the main page has student life and academics, when both can be placed under a similar banner such as Current Students. Which is ironic, because the student life tab is the Current Students tab. Stupid, it's pointlessly misleading. Also there are multiple rows of the same info on different pages. For example Academics and Student Life/Current Students both have the same Academic Support row. Also the only way to get to the main home page is to click the logo. And on the main page, the navigation bar that is normally in the right hand corner is moved to under the sub-navigation, yet another visibility issue.
