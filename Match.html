Match Between System and the Real World


"The system should speak the users' language, with words, phrases and concepts familiar to the user, rather than system-oriented terms. Follow real-world conventions, making information appear in a natural and logical order." - Nielsen


The semantics that this site uses are very basic, the general public should not have an issue understanding what the links or information mean. There is really no need for clarification on any part of the site. Although when I reviewed the site, there were several references to Ragin' Cajuns, originally I had no idea what that was. I think that maybe for the parents who aren't to interested in sports or school spirit, the mascot should be more prominently explained so that they aren't confused.

Also on the About Us page, the SACS-COC accreditation is just a confusing link, I have no idea what SACS-COC is, nor do I want to go out of my way to find out what it stands for. In other words, as a user I don't want to have to deal with acronyms, however as the only link that wasn't clear I would say that is a pretty decent Match to World.
